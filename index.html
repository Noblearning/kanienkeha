About this content & its foundations Vocabulary drawn from the curriculum by Onwá:ri Tekahawáhkwen Mary McDonald, Mohawk Language Custodian Association (Kontinónhstats), Kanehsatà:ke — home of the oldest spoken Mohawk dialect. kanehsatakevoices.com ↗

This app draws on critical language pedagogy from García & Li Wei (translanguaging), Flores & Rosa (raciolinguistics), McCarty & Lee (culturally sustaining/revitalizing pedagogy), Freire (literacy as liberation), and Chew, Leonard & Rosenblum (decolonizing Indigenous language pedagogies). All dialect variants are treated as equally valid — see any word entry for community attribution.

A note on dialect: This guide reflects the Kanehsatà:ke dialect (oldest spoken Mohawk dialect). Other communities use different but equally valid forms — e.g. Akwesasne uses l where Kanehsatà:ke uses r; Six Nations/Tyendinaga use y where Kanehsatà:ke uses i. No dialect is more "correct" than another. Learn more about dialect variation ↗
🔊 Audio note: The play buttons below use your device's text-to-speech engine to approximate each sound. No browser currently has a Kanien'kéha voice — these clips use carefully chosen phonetic approximations designed to produce the closest possible sound. They are a starting guide only. Always prioritise audio from community speakers at kanehsatakevoices.com ↗ and FirstVoices ↗.
